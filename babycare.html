Nutrition Corner

0-6 Months: Feeding Basics

Exclusive breastfeeding is recommended for the first 6 months. Formula is a healthy alternative.

  • Feed on demand, typically every 2-3 hours for newborns.
  • Look for hunger cues like rooting, lip smacking, or putting hands to mouth.
  • Ensure baby is latching correctly if breastfeeding.
  • Burp baby during and after feedings.
Starting Solids (Around 6 Months)

Introduce single-ingredient purees when baby shows signs of readiness (good head control, interest in food).

  • Start with iron-rich foods like fortified cereals or pureed meats.
  • Introduce new foods one at a time, waiting 2-3 days before adding another.
  • Offer a variety of fruits, vegetables, and grains over time.
  • Avoid honey before age 1 and limit added salt or sugar.

BabyCare Myth Busters

Myth: You can spoil a newborn by holding them too much.

Fact: You cannot spoil a young baby with affection. Holding, cuddling, and responding to your baby's needs helps build security and attachment. It's crucial for their emotional development.

Myth: Adding cereal to a baby's bottle helps them sleep longer.

Fact: This is generally not recommended and can be a choking hazard. It doesn't significantly improve sleep and can lead to overfeeding. Introduce solids with a spoon when developmentally appropriate.

Myth: Baby walkers help babies learn to walk faster.

Fact: Baby walkers can actually delay walking and pose significant safety risks (falls, access to hazards). Stationary activity centers are a safer alternative if desired.
